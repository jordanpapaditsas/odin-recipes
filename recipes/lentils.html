Lentil Soup

Greek Lentil Soup

Lentil soup is a soup with lentils as its main ingredient; it may be vegetarian or include meat, and may use brown, red, yellow, green or black lentils, with or without the husk. Dehulled yellow and red lentils disintegrate in cooking, making a thick soup.

Ingredients

  • 250 g lentils
  • 2 tablespoon(s) olive oil
  • 1 onion, medium
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 stick(s) celery
  • 2 clove(s) of garlic
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 pinch chili flakes
  • 400 g canned tomatoes
  • 1 1/2 liter water
  • 1 teaspoon(s) salt

Steps

  1. Rinse the lentils and drain.
  2. Place a large pot over medium heat.
  3. Coarsely chop the onion and add them to the pot along with the olive oil.
  4. Chop the carrots and celery into small slices.
  5. Add them to the pot and saut√© for 3-5 minutes, until they soften.
  6. Thinly slice the garlic and add to the pot along with the bay leaves, and chili flakes
  7. Stir and add the lentils, chopped tomatoes and vegetable stock.
  8. Cover the pot and simmer for 45 minutes over medium to low heat.
  9. When ready, add the salt. (The salt is not added at the beginning so that the lentils don't harden.)
  10. Serve the lentils in a serving bowl and add 1 tablespoon of olive oil and 1 tablespoon of vinegar.
